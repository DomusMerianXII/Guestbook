Our Favorite Places

Trier is a city of history for many — but for us, it is also a city full of special moments. Over time, we have discovered places here that touch us, excite us, relax us, or simply make us happy.

Here we share our very personal favorite places with you — not the typical tourist spots, but small gems, special views, honest food, and authentic Trier lifestyle.

Whether it’s a glass of wine at the Hauptmarkt, the scent of extraordinary perfumes, or a sunset over the rooftops — this is our Trier. Maybe it will become a bit of yours too.

Enjoy discovering –
Jana & Family


Gut Avelsbach

A little gem in Trier: Riesling straight from the winemaker, a cozy wine garden, and blues mini-concerts on Saturdays. Perfect for relaxed afternoons in good company.

Parfümerie Lücke

A true scent experience: extraordinary niche perfumes, lovingly selected cosmetics, and an ambiance that makes you want more. Ideal for a touch of luxury between study sessions.

HONG&FRIENDS

Creative studio, cult shop, and screen printing workshop in Saarstraße — with Hong, Johannes, exceptional designs, fair trade textiles, and lots of heart.

Brasserie SIM

Right by the Porta Nigra — French-German cuisine, relaxed atmosphere, regional & seasonal. For us, always the perfect restart after a long week.

Früh bis spät

Rustic Kölsch brewery with top-quality steaks (up to 750 g!) and the feeling of meeting friends “the Kölsch way.” For us, a place that’s just effortlessly cool.

Wine Stand at Hauptmarkt

Right on the square: Mosel, Saar, and Ruwer wines by the glass. Two changing winemakers every week, no frills — a lived Moselle wine festival!

Flietenfranz

For fried chicken wings with amazing seasoning and BBQ vibes. Right in the middle and simply different — a hidden gem for fans of sides like fries and smoky aromas.

Petrisberg

Perfect spot for sunsets: small picnic, peaceful view over sparkling Trier. Just arrive, breathe out, and marvel.

Mariensäule

A viewpoint with history: about 40 meters above the city, with views over Moselle loops and rooftops — always surprisingly beautiful.
